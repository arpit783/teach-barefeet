1Mission
Each day we wake up from our beds to bring out new smiles on the face of innocents. Interaction is the key. We socialize with underprivilged children, teach them basic courses along with how to think and act in order to achieve overall development we all learn from our schools.
2Process
Process is as simple as it seems. We focus on 'each one teach one' to give special sttention to each child that they deserve in order to bring out the best of them. Education imparted covers all its components: inculcation, moral development, analysis, values clarification, and action learning.
3Vision
Our team believes that if one privileged child would give a little bit of time to educate an underprivileged child, our nation will witness an educated and not just literate society in near future. India has a very high volume of child trafficking. As many as one child disappears every eight minutes according to NCRB. We believe in changing one life at a time, and making change from the grassroot.

    Issues that Underprivileged Children Have to Deal With

    India is the seventh largest country in the world and boasts of being a rapidly growing economy, yet its underprivileged children still have to experience some of the worst living conditions on the globe. These helpless children have to deal with various issues like malnutrition, illiteracy, child mortality and abuse due to the financial circumstances of their parents and family members. These hardships end up robbing the children of the opportunities to grow into a successful and independent adult.

    Help Street Children Fulfill their Needs and Dreams

    Before they were street children, India’s children were ordinary, happy young kids, abandoned at a young age, or dragged to the cities for labour. Among the many challenges that India’s street children face every day, the three most significant are access to nutrition, education, and safety. Guaranteeing these as essentials will go a long away in achieving a future of socio-economic improvement, and empowerment for India’s street children.
